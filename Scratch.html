It's Learning Time!

Aloha! I’m Julia and I love art of all kinds! On this page, we’ll get to learn about a bunch of different types of art, and I’m gonna share some fun facts and food for thought about art.

If you'd like to look at more art, take a look at my Poetry Portfolio.

  1. Museums
    • Art Museums are some of the best places we can go to appreciate art!
    • Not only are they a place of great inspiration, but also a place to learn history and observe artist’s techniques.
    • Art Museums are very important to me because ever since I was little.
      My mom would bring my siblings and I to all kinds of museums.
      Her motto was “There’s always something to learn”, and that fuels my passion for learning.
  2. Jewelry
    • Jewelry is an art form I picked up a few years ago, and I adore it!
    • Something unique about making art with metals is its longevity–a well-made piece will last hundreds of years!
    • However, the small size and application of jewelry allow us to keep art with meaning close to us.
  3. Glass
    • Glass art will always be one of my favorite art mediums!
    • From windows to vases to coasters, glass art is unique because of its transparency and ability to manipulate light.
    • A fun fact: Did you know the main factor in determining glass price is color?
    • It's because different colors require different elements to be mixed with the glass. For example, red glass has gold in it!
  4. Drawing
    • Drawing is an amazing form of art, not only because of its wide applications but also because of its accessibility.
    • From digital drawings to ink and paper, to cave walls, drawing is something anyone can pick up!
    • In my opinion, drawings are most often a gift for someone. Handmade cards are the biggest example of this.
  5. Music
    • Music is a very special art form because you don't need eyes to see it.
    • Scientifically, people, plants, and animals react to music. For us humans, certain tones and melodies cause us to release dopamine.
      For plants, calm instrumental music like classical or Guoyue increases their growth and strength.
    • Music is also very important for culture. Every culture has a distinct sound and stories carried through music.
      For example, My class sang the Hawaiian Mele: Na Kuahiwi Kaulana last year.
TV Entertainment is art too! Take a look at Disney's historical stock rates.
